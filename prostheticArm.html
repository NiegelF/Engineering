Prosthetic Arm Description

I fashioned this prosthetic arm utilizing an Arduino Nano, MG996R servo, and an EMG sensor. Straying from the intricacies of Fusion 360, my design took shape in TinkerCAD during those early days. Tying the tendons together, I opted for fishing wire and reinforced the structure with kevlar cord to ensure the fingers maintained their dexterity. Embarking on such an ambitious project from the get-go was undoubtedly daring, but with diligence, I managed to bring it to operational fruition. Looking back, the journey underscores the significant learning curve encountered, such that were I to undertake a similar project today, the timeline would assuredly be notably condensed. It serves as a vivid testament to the substantial growth and knowledge garnered from this transformative experience.
