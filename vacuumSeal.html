Vacuum Seal Wall Climber Description

Allow me to elaborate on the development of my vacuum seal wall climber, a project conceived to leverage a 12V air pump for creating a vacuum seal within a chamber, controllable via a switch. The crux of the endeavor involved meticulous calculations to ensure that the surface area, in conjunction with the -11 psi output of the air pump, generated sufficient force to support my body weight. Navigating through a series of iterations, I initially employed 3D printed versions. Regrettably, these iterations proved inadequate in terms of both stability and force generation. Recognizing the need for a more robust solution, I transitioned to a larger wooden design with a foam base, intending to fortify the structural integrity of the chamber. The calculations necessitated a careful consideration of atmospheric pressure, my body weight, and the targeted force required for adherence to the wall. Subsequent to numerous trial-and-error phases, the ultimate design emerged, capable of effectively supporting my weight. The evolution of the project involved overcoming challenges associated with the limitations of 3D printing, leading to the adoption of a more substantial wooden framework. To sum it up, the vacuum seal wall climber project encapsulates a journey of calculated design decisions, iterative development, and a persistent pursuit of an optimal solution.
